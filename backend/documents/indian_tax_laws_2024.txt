# Indian Income Tax Laws - Financial Year 2024-25

## Tax Regime Options

### Old Tax Regime
The old tax regime allows various deductions and exemptions under different sections of the Income Tax Act.

**Tax Slabs (Old Regime for FY 2024-25):**
- Up to ₹2,50,000: NIL
- ₹2,50,001 to ₹5,00,000: 5%
- ₹5,00,001 to ₹10,00,000: 20%
- Above ₹10,00,000: 30%

**Available Deductions under Old Regime:**
- Section 80C: Up to ₹1,50,000 (EPF, PPF, ELSS, Life Insurance, NSC, SSY, etc.)
- Section 80CCD(1B): Additional ₹50,000 for NPS contributions
- Section 80D: Health Insurance Premium (₹25,000 for self, ₹50,000 for senior citizen parents)
- Section 80TTA/80TTB: Savings account interest (₹10,000/₹50,000 for senior citizens)
- Section 24: Home Loan Interest up to ₹2,00,000
- Standard Deduction: ₹50,000 for salaried individuals
- HRA Exemption: Based on actual rent paid and salary structure

### New Tax Regime (Default from FY 2023-24)
The new tax regime offers lower tax rates but removes most deductions and exemptions.

**Tax Slabs (New Regime for FY 2024-25):**
- Up to ₹3,00,000: NIL
- ₹3,00,001 to ₹7,00,000: 5%
- ₹7,00,001 to ₹10,00,000: 10%
- ₹10,00,001 to ₹12,00,000: 15%
- ₹12,00,001 to ₹15,00,000: 20%
- Above ₹15,00,000: 30%

**Limited Deductions in New Regime:**
- Standard Deduction: ₹75,000 (increased in Budget 2024)
- NPS Employer Contribution: Section 80CCD(2) up to 14% of salary
- Gratuity, Leave Encashment exemptions continue

## Section 80C Investments

### Public Provident Fund (PPF)
- Maximum investment: ₹1,50,000 per year
- Lock-in period: 15 years (partial withdrawal after 7 years)
- Interest Rate: 7.1% (as of FY 2024-25, revised quarterly)
- Tax Treatment: EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt)
- Eligibility: Indian residents only

### Employee Provident Fund (EPF)
- Employee contribution: 12% of basic salary
- Employer contribution: 12% (3.67% to EPF, 8.33% to EPS)
- Tax-free interest on contributions up to ₹2,50,000 per year

### Equity Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS)
- Minimum investment: ₹500
- Lock-in period: 3 years (shortest among 80C options)
- Returns: Market-linked (historically 12-15% average)
- Tax on gains: 10% LTCG above ₹1,00,000

### Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)
- Eligibility: Girl child below 10 years
- Maximum investment: ₹1,50,000 per year
- Interest Rate: 8.2% (highest among small savings)
- Maturity: 21 years from account opening or marriage after 18

### National Savings Certificate (NSC)
- Lock-in period: 5 years
- Interest Rate: 7.7%
- Minimum investment: ₹1,000

## Section 80D - Health Insurance

**Premium Limits:**
- Self, spouse, children: Up to ₹25,000 (₹50,000 if senior citizen)
- Parents: Additional ₹25,000 (₹50,000 if senior citizen)
- Maximum deduction: ₹1,00,000 (if both self and parents are senior citizens)

**Preventive Health Checkup:**
- Additional ₹5,000 (within overall limit)

## National Pension System (NPS)

### Section 80CCD(1)
- Part of overall 80C limit of ₹1,50,000
- Employee contribution up to 10% of salary

### Section 80CCD(1B)
- Additional deduction of ₹50,000
- Available over and above 80C limit
- Total tax benefit: Up to ₹2,00,000 (80C + 80CCD(1B))

### Section 80CCD(2)
- Employer contribution to NPS
- Up to 14% of salary (Central Govt) or 10% of salary (others)
- No upper limit in rupees
- Available in BOTH old and new tax regime

### NPS Withdrawal Rules
- 60% can be withdrawn tax-free at retirement
- 40% must be used to buy annuity (pension)
- Partial withdrawal: Up to 25% for specific purposes after 3 years

## Home Loan Tax Benefits

### Section 24 - Interest on Home Loan
- Self-occupied property: Up to ₹2,00,000 per year
- Let-out property: No upper limit (entire interest is deductible)
- Construction must be completed within 5 years

### Section 80EEA - Additional Interest Benefit
- First-time home buyers
- Stamp duty value up to ₹45 lakh
- Loan sanctioned between April 2019 to March 2022
- Additional deduction: Up to ₹1,50,000

### Section 80C - Principal Repayment
- Part of overall ₹1,50,000 limit
- Includes stamp duty and registration charges (in year of payment)

## Capital Gains Tax

### Short Term Capital Gains (STCG)
- Equity (held < 12 months): 15%
- Other assets (held < 24/36 months): As per income tax slab

### Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG)
- Equity (held > 12 months): 10% above ₹1,00,000
- Other assets: 20% with indexation benefit
- Property: 20% with indexation benefit

### Section 54 - Exemption on Property Sale
- Reinvest LTCG in new residential property
- Purchase: 1 year before or 2 years after sale
- Construction: 3 years after sale
- Maximum exemption: Entire LTCG amount

## Tax Filing Deadlines

- ITR for non-audit cases: 31st July
- ITR for audit cases: 31st October
- Belated return: 31st December (with penalty)
- Revised return: 31st December

## TDS Rates (Key Categories)

- Salary: As per slab rates
- Bank Interest: 10% (if exceeds ₹40,000/₹50,000 for seniors)
- Rent: 10% (if rent exceeds ₹2,40,000 per year)
- Professional Fees: 10%
- Property Sale: 1% (if value exceeds ₹50 lakh)

## Tax Saving Tips for Different Income Levels

### Income ₹10-15 Lakh
1. Maximize 80C through PPF/ELSS: ₹1,50,000
2. NPS 80CCD(1B): ₹50,000
3. Health Insurance 80D: ₹25,000-₹75,000
4. Compare old vs new regime (old often better with deductions)

### Income ₹15-25 Lakh
1. All above deductions
2. Consider home loan for Section 24 benefit
3. Utilize employer NPS contribution
4. Plan long-term investments for LTCG exemption

### Income Above ₹25 Lakh
1. Maximize all available deductions
2. Structure salary with tax-efficient components
3. Consider HUF formation for additional benefits
4. Plan charitable contributions under 80G
